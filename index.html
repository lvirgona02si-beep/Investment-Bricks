Common Questions

Duplex Investment — FAQ

What exactly is a duplex investment property? +
A duplex is a single land parcel containing two separate, self-contained dwellings — each with its own entrance, kitchen, and living areas. As an investor, you receive rental income from both dwellings, effectively doubling your income stream compared to a standard investment property on the same block.
Is duplex development legal across all Australian states? +
Yes, duplex development is legal across all Australian states, but planning rules vary by council. Key considerations include minimum lot size, R-code zoning, and setback requirements. Our roadmap outlines the state-by-state rules in detail so you know exactly where the most investor-friendly opportunities exist right now.
How much capital do I need to start a duplex investment? +
Most of our clients use a combination of equity from an existing property and cash savings. Generally, investors with $200k+ in available equity or deposit funds are well-positioned to get started. We'll assess your specific situation during a strategy call and show you exactly what's achievable.
What kind of rental yields can I expect from a duplex? +
Duplex investments in strong-demand corridors are currently delivering gross rental yields of 5.5–7.5% in many markets, compared to 3.5–4.5% for comparable single-dwelling properties. The roadmap includes real examples of current Investment Bricks projects with detailed yield breakdowns.
Do I need to manage the properties myself? +
No — Investment Bricks provides end-to-end facilitation including connections to professional property managers who specialise in dual-occupancy properties. Most of our investors are fully hands-off from the moment tenants move in.
What is the difference between a duplex and a dual-key property? +
A duplex has two fully separate dwellings on one title (or two separate titles after subdivision), while a dual-key property is technically one dwelling with an internal connecting door. Duplexes typically offer stronger rental yields and more flexibility — including the option to sell each dwelling independently after subdivision.
