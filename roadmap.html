Why Duplexes — Why Now?

Three converging forces are creating the most favourable conditions for duplex investment in Australia's modern history: a structural housing undersupply, record rental demand, and a federal government mandate to build more dwellings faster — particularly medium-density housing like duplexes.

Australia's population is growing at its fastest rate in decades — 423,600 people were added in the year to September 2025 alone. Queensland, Western Australia, and Victoria are absorbing the bulk of this growth. Each new resident needs somewhere to live. Rental vacancy rates — already below 1.5% nationally — remain at crisis levels in most capital cities and regional centres, keeping vacancy rates low and rents high.

The gap is widening. Dwelling completions are running well below the National Housing Accord's 1.2 million target. State governments have responded by fast-tracking medium-density approvals — particularly dual-occupancy and duplex developments — making this the most supportive planning environment for duplex investors in a generation.

Against this backdrop, a duplex investment delivers something a standard investment property simply cannot: two separate, self-contained rental incomes from one parcel of land. When vacancy rates are low, both dwellings tend to remain tenanted simultaneously — compounding the income advantage and reducing your effective vacancy risk.

Chapter 2 · Understanding the Duplex Strategy
Chapter 02
Understanding the Strategy

A duplex is a single land parcel containing two fully self-contained dwellings — each with its own entrance, kitchen, living areas, bedrooms, and bathrooms. They may sit side-by-side (most common) or one on top of the other. Both dwellings sit on one title initially and can often be subdivided into two separate strata titles after completion, giving investors the flexibility to hold both or sell one.

Key Advantages
  • Reduced vacancy risk: Even if one dwelling is vacant, the other continues generating income — unlike a single dwelling that produces $0 when empty.
  • Higher gross yields: Two rents from one land cost. Duplex investors in growth corridors regularly achieve 6–8% gross yields vs. 3.5–4.5% for standalone houses.
  • Stronger depreciation: New construction across two dwellings means double the capital works (Div. 43) and plant & equipment (Div. 40) depreciation claims.
  • Subdivision upside: Once construction is complete, many duplexes can be strata-titled, giving you the option to sell one dwelling and recycle capital — or hold both for long-term compounding.
  • Medium-density planning support: State and federal governments are actively fast-tracking medium-density approvals, shortening DA timelines for complying duplex developments.

Chapter 4 · Finding the Right Site
Chapter 04
Finding the Right Site

Site selection is the single most important decision in a duplex development. Not every block of land that meets minimum lot size requirements will stack up financially — and not every suburb with affordability will have the rental demand to sustain two tenants simultaneously. Here is what Investment Bricks looks for in every site assessment.

Must-Have Criteria
  • Correct zoning for dual occupancy (check council DA requirements)
  • Minimum lot size met for the specific council (typically 600–700sqm)
  • Appropriate frontage (usually 15–18m minimum for side-by-side)
  • No significant flood, bushfire, or acid sulphate soil overlays
  • Within 1km of schools, services, and transport corridors
  • Rental vacancy below 2% in the suburb/postcode
  • Population growth trend in the region
  • Comparable duplex rents demonstrable from recent leases
Red Flags to Avoid
  • Heritage overlays that restrict demolition or new builds
  • Sites requiring retaining walls >2m (cost blowout risk)
  • Lots with irregular shape reducing usable area
  • Suburb vacancy rates above 3%
  • Overland flow paths across the building envelope
  • Councils with DA backlogs exceeding 6 months
  • Sites priced at full development uplift (no margin left)

Chapter 5 · Finance Structures
Chapter 05
Finance Structures

Duplex construction is typically financed through a land and construction loan — a two-stage facility where the land is purchased first, then the construction component draws down progressively as the build reaches key milestones. Understanding this structure is essential to managing your cash flow throughout the project.

Finance Pathway How It Works Best For Key Consideration
Cash + Construction Loan Cash deposit for land; bank funds build progress payments First-time duplex investors with 20%+ deposit Interest-only during construction; full repayments post-completion
Equity Release (PPOR) Draw equity from existing home as deposit; then construction loan Existing homeowners with 50%+ equity in PPOR No cash required out of pocket; equity does the work
Investment Portfolio Equity Cross-collateralise or release equity from existing IP Experienced investors growing a portfolio Maintain clean loan structures; avoid cross-collateralisation where possible
SMSF Borrowing Limited recourse borrowing arrangement (LRBA) within superannuation Self-managed super fund trustees Strict rules apply; specialist SMSF lender required; 70–75% LVR typical

Tax tip — new build depreciation: A $560,000 duplex build typically generates $14,000–$19,000/year in ATO-approved depreciation deductions across Division 43 (capital works, 2.5%/yr) and Division 40 (plant and equipment). At a 37% marginal tax rate, this can save $5,000–$7,000 in tax annually — effectively subsidising your holding costs.

Borrowing capacity rule of thumb: Most lenders will accept 80% of the projected rental income from both dwellings in their serviceability assessment — a significant uplift over the single-income assessment applied to a standard investment property. This alone can increase your borrowing capacity considerably.

🔥 Top Pick: Southeast Queensland

SEQ continues to attract interstate migration at record levels. Ipswich and Moreton Bay council areas have dual-occupancy-friendly codes, land prices still below the Melbourne/Sydney equivalents, and rental yields that make the numbers work. Population growth of 97,000+ per year sustains rental demand across both dwellings simultaneously.

🔥 Rising Market: Perth, WA

Western Australia is Australia's fastest growing state at 2.2% per annum. Perth's outer-ring suburbs have R-code zoning that supports duplex development, land prices remain relatively affordable, and rental vacancy sits at crisis lows — pushing rents and yields higher. A compelling combination for investors seeking income and growth.

Steady: Adelaide, SA

Adelaide's outer northern and southern suburbs offer strong duplex yields with the lowest entry costs of any capital city. PlanSA's medium-density policy has simplified approvals in targeted growth zones. Ideal for investors with a lower capital budget seeking reliable yield-first returns.

Note: Victoria & NSW

Higher land costs in Melbourne and Sydney compress gross yields relative to QLD and WA. That said, outer-ring opportunities exist in both states — particularly in Melton (VIC) and Penrith/Campbelltown (NSW) — where land affordability and strong population growth make duplex projects viable for investors focused on long-term equity.

Also Serviced: Tasmania

Investment Bricks services Tasmania across Hobart, Launceston, Devonport, and Burnie. Tasmania offers some of the most affordable duplex entry points of any Australian market, with Hobart's rental vacancy consistently below 1% — among the tightest in the country. Interstate migration driven by lifestyle and affordability continues to underpin rental demand. Outer Hobart suburbs (Glenorchy, Clarence, Kingston) and Launceston's inner ring present strong yield-first opportunities for investors seeking lower entry costs without sacrificing income performance. The Tasmanian Planning Scheme has streamlined dual-occupancy approvals in residential zones, making project delivery more predictable than in some mainland jurisdictions.
